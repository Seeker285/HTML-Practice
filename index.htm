RGB, HEX, HSL, RGBA, and HSLA all use different value types to assign a color:

  • RGB: Uses red, green, and blue to determine the final color.

    rgb(120, 87, 0) creates a shade of brown.

  • HEX: Uses a hexadecimal value to determine the final color, with a # preceding it. The format is #rrggbb where red (rr), green (gg) and blue (bb) are displayed by a value between 00-ff (ff being the highest).

    #000888 creates a shade of green.

  • HSL: Uses hue, saturation, and lightness to determine the final color. Hue is degree on the color wheel, 0-360. Red is 0, 120 is green, and 240 is blue. Saturation is the percentage of color value, with 0% being a shade of gray and 100% being the full color. Lastly, lightness is a percentage of brightness, with 0% being black and 100% being white.

    hsl(200, 95%, 25%) creates a shade of blue.

  • RGBA: Uses the same red, green, and blue parameters as RGB, but with the addition of alpha. Alpha affects the transparency of the color on a scale of 0.0 (fully transparent) to 1.0 (fully opaque).

    rgba(120, 87, 0, 0.5) is a transparent brown.

  • HSLA: Uses the same hue, saturation, and lightness parameters as HSL, but with the addition of alpha. Alpha affects the transparency of the color on a scale of 0.0 (fully transparent) to 1.0 (fully opaque).

    hsla(200, 95%, 25%, 0.5) is a transparent blue.
